The BLUE LOCK Project

You're invited by the JFU to a hyper-intensive soccer training camp. Will you accept?

You arrive at the Japanese Football Union's building, where you learn about the "BLUE LOCK" project. The project leader talks about what it means to be the best forward position player in the world, and how being egotistical plays into that. He has many controversial takes, but your gut instinct agrees with his words.

You decide that you don't want to waste your time by checking it out, and you continue playing for your school's team.

After you spearhead the charge towards the buses that'll take you to Blue Lock, many other high schoolers join your charge to join the project. This is the beginning of your training to become the best in the world.

Some time passes, and you hear that the Blue Lock participants are being very successful, and are going to face off against the Japanese National U-20 team. You imagine what your abilities would be like if you decided to go. What a shame.
